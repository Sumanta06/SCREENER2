MCX Trading Manual: 6 Key Indicators

(Reference Image above)

1. Support & Resistance (Market Range Gauge)

This blue bar is your most important map. It shows you exactly where the current market price is standing.

  • Support (The Floor): This is the left side of the gauge. This is where buyers usually step in and bounce the price up.
  • Resistance (The Ceiling): This is the right side of the gauge. This is where sellers usually step in and push the price down.

How to Trade using the Black Needle:

  • Needle near the Left (Support): If the black needle is very close to Support, your downside risk is very low. This is the safest and best place to Buy Calls (Go Long). Your stop-loss can be kept very small (just below the Support number).
  • Needle near the Right (Resistance): If the black needle is very close to Resistance, the market will struggle to go higher. You should book your Call profits here. Alternatively, if the main Radar says "Sell", this is the best place to Buy Puts (Go Short).
  • Needle in the Middle (50% mark): The market is exactly in "no man's land." The risk is 50-50 on both sides. Be very careful taking new trades here.

2. PCR (Put Call Ratio)

This shows the overall mood of the market.

  • Above 1.0 = Bullish (Good for buying Calls).
  • Below 1.0 = Bearish (Good for buying Puts).
  • Above 1.5 (OVERBOUGHT): ⚠️ Danger! The market has gone up too fast and might suddenly drop. Avoid buying new Calls.
  • Below 0.5 (OVERSOLD): ⚠️ Danger! The market has fallen too fast and might suddenly bounce back up. Avoid buying new Puts.

3. Max Pain

Think of Max Pain as a giant "Magnet".

Big players (Option Sellers) make their maximum profit if the market closes exactly at this price. (Remember: Option Seller's profit means Option Buyer's loss!) As Expiry gets closer, the market price will act like it is being pulled toward this Magnet. If the current price is very far above or below Max Pain, expect the price to slowly be pulled back toward this number.

4. Imbalance (Immediate Sentiment)

Think of this gauge as a live "Traffic Light" for your entry. It shows who is controlling the market right at this very second.

Red Zone (More Calls): The market is facing immediate selling pressure. STOP. Even if the main Radar says "Buy", do not buy yet. Wait for the pressure to clear.

Green Zone (More Puts): The market is getting immediate buying support. GO. If your main Radar says "Buy" and this gauge is also Green, it is perfectly safe to enter.

5. Delta (Option Chain)

Think of Delta as the "Speed Meter" of your option.

Simple Example: If you buy an Option that has a Delta of 0.50, it means if the main market moves up by ₹1, your Option price will move up by ₹0.50 (50 Paise).

Golden Rule: Always choose a Strike Price with a Delta between 0.40 and 0.60. If you buy an option with a very small Delta (like 0.10), your premium will barely increase even if the market goes up!

6. Theta (Option Chain)

Think of Theta as an "Ice Cube Melting".

Every single day, the value of your Option slowly melts away (loses value) just because of passing time. This happens even if the market doesn't move at all.

  • Simple Example: If you look at the Option Chain and see a Theta of -0.39, it means that specific Option will lose exactly ₹0.39 (39 Paise) of its value every single day, just sitting there!
  • For Option Buyers: Theta is your enemy. If you buy an option and the market stays flat, you will lose money every day just by holding it.
  • For Option Sellers: Theta is their friend. They make money purely from this daily melting effect.

Golden Rule: Never buy options if you think the market is going to be sideways or flat!

Part 2: OI Compare Terminal (Your Time Machine)

(Reference Image above)

1. T1 and T2 (Time Selection)

The OI Compare terminal allows you to look into the past and see exactly what big players were doing between any two specific times during the day.

  • T1 (Past Time): The starting time you want to check.
  • T2 (End Time): The ending time you want to check.

How to Use: If the market suddenly crashed between 10:00 AM and 10:30 AM, you can set T1=10:00 and T2=10:30 to investigate exactly who caused the crash!

2. BIAS (Overall Result)

This box instantly calculates all the complex data between T1 and T2 and gives you the final result.

  • If it says BEARISH (Red), it means big players heavily sold Calls during that time (creating heavy downward pressure).
  • If it says BULLISH (Green), it means big players heavily sold Puts during that time (creating strong upward support).

3. Δ OI (Change in Open Interest)

This column is the secret weapon. Always read this from the Option Seller's (Big Player's) perspective.

  • Positive Numbers (+): Option Sellers are confidently selling and adding new contracts. They are building strong Resistance (if on Call side) or strong Support (if on Put side).
  • Negative Red Numbers (-) [Short Covering]: Option Sellers are getting trapped and buying back their positions in a panic to stop their losses!
    • If Call Sellers run away (huge negative CE numbers), the ceiling breaks, and the market will fly UP.
    • If Put Sellers run away (huge negative PE numbers), the floor breaks, and the market will crash DOWN.
